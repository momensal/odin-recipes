Shawarma

Description

This authentic Middle Eastern lamb shawarma is deeply marinated in yogurt, lemon, warm spices, and garlic, then slow-cooked or grilled until tender and bursting with flavor. Perfect for stuffing into warm pita, drizzling over hummus, topping fattoush, or enjoying on its own — bold, aromatic, and built to impress.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup plain yogurt
  • ¼ cup water
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon distilled white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • ½ cup chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ¾ teaspoon ground mace
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 5 pounds boneless lamb shoulder, cut into 1/4-inch-thick strips

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, water, lemon juice, vinegar, olive oil, chopped onion, and minced garlic.
  2. Whisk in the salt, black pepper, cumin, nutmeg, clove, mace, and cayenne until fully blended.
  3. Add the lamb strips and mix until they’re fully coated.
  4. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and marinate in the fridge for 12 to 24 hours — the longer, the better.
  5. When ready to cook, heat a large skillet over high heat.
  6. Working in batches, cook the lamb in a single layer for about 5 minutes per batch, turning occasionally, until browned and cooked through.
