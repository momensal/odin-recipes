Loaded Fries

Description

Smoky, char-grilled skirt steak marinated in a bold citrus-chipotle blend, cooked directly over hot lump charcoal for that primal sear. Sliced and stacked over a bed of crispy waffle fries, drenched in creamy queso, then topped with fire-roasted salsa, fresh guac, sour cream, chopped red onions, and cilantro. A wild fusion of street food energy and backyard heat — loud, messy, unforgettable.

Ingredients

  • 1-2 large Skirt Steaks
  • 1/4 cup Cilantro
  • 4 Garlic Cloves
  • 3 medium Limes juiced
  • 2 Navel Oranges juiced
  • 2.5 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 2 tbsp Green Onions
  • 1.5 tbsp Chipotle Puree
  • 1 tbsp Achiote Paste
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 Red Bell Pepper
  • 1 Roma Tomato
  • 1/2 Red Onion
  • 1 Jalapeño
  • 3-4 Garlic Cloves
  • 1/4 cup White Vinegar
  • 3 medium Limes juiced
  • Kosher Salt to taste
  • 2 lbs Waffle Fries of your choosing or homemade
  • 2 cups Queso of your choosing or homemade
  • Guacamole
  • Sour Cream
  • Chopped Cilantro
  • Chopped Red Onions

Steps

  1. Start by throwing all the marinade ingredients into a blender or food processor and blend it till smooth. Drop your skirt steaks into a food-safe container, pour the marinade all over, cover it, and stick it in the fridge for at least 4 hours — overnight is best.
  2. Next day, fire up some high heat Cowboy lump charcoal. Let it build into a pile and get about 75% white hot before you cook.
  3. Blow off any loose ash, then lay your steaks right on the coals. Cook 4–6 min per side, flipping more if the coals start to char the meat. Blow off ash every time you flip. If coals get stuck, just brush them off. Once internal hits 120°F, pull them off and rest for 10 min.
  4. While the steaks rest, toss your tomato, bell pepper, onion, and jalapeño straight into the coals to char for 3–4 min. Pull them, clean off the skins/stems/seeds, and throw them in a food processor with the rest of the salsa ingredients. Blend, pour into a bowl, done.
  5. Now slice your steak against the grain into bite-sized pieces. Build your fries: spread them out, pour on the queso, stack the steak, salsa, guac, sour cream, chopped red onions, and cilantro on top. Serve it up.
